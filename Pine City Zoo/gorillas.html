Elephants are the largest existing land animals, known for their intelligence, memory, and strong social bonds. The family Elephantidae is divided into three species: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant. Elephants are easily recognized by their trunk, large ears, and tusks.

  • Elephants are native to Africa and Asia, inhabiting savannas, forests, deserts, and marshes.
  • African bush elephants are the largest and are found in various habitats across sub-Saharan Africa.
  • African forest elephants are smaller and live in the dense forests of Central and West Africa.
  • Asian elephants are found in grasslands and forests across South and Southeast Asia.
  • Elephants play a crucial role in their ecosystems by modifying habitats and dispersing seeds.
