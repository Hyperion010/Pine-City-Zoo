KOALAS

The elephant (Loxodonta africana and Elephas maximus) is the largest existing land animal. Elephants are known for their intelligence, strong social bonds, and distinctive trunks.

  • Native to Africa and Asia, with African elephants found in sub-Saharan Africa and Asian elephants in South and Southeast Asia.
  • Easily recognised by their massive bodies, large ears (especially in African species), and long, muscular trunks.
  • Body length: up to 7.5 m (24 ft); Weight: up to 6,000 kg (13,200 lb).
  • Thick, grey skin with sparse hair and large, curved tusks (in most males and some females).
  • African elephants are larger with bigger ears, while Asian elephants are smaller with rounded ears.

Elephants inhabit savannas, forests, deserts, and marshes. They are herbivores, feeding on grasses, leaves, bark, and fruit. Elephants are highly social, living in matriarchal family groups and communicating with a variety of vocalisations and body language.

  • Live in herds led by the oldest female, known as the matriarch.
  • Strong bonds between family members; young are cared for by the entire herd.
  • Use their trunks for feeding, drinking, social interaction, and bathing.
  • Gestation lasts about 22 months, the longest of any land mammal.
  • Threatened by habitat loss and poaching for ivory.
