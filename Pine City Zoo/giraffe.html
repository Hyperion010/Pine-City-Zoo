GIRAFFE

Giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis) is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. Its long neck, legs, and unique coat patterns make it easy to recognize. Giraffes belong to the family Giraffidae, along with the okapi. There are nine subspecies, each with different coat patterns.

Giraffes are found from Chad in the north to South Africa in the south, and from Niger in the west to Somalia in the east. They live in savannahs, grasslands, and open woodlands.

Their main food is acacia leaves, which they can reach thanks to their height.

Lions are the main predators of giraffes. Calves may also be hunted by leopards, spotted hyenas, and African wild dogs.

Giraffes do not form strong social groups, but may gather in loose herds. Males fight for dominance by swinging their necks, and the winners get to mate with females.
